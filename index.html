Invasive Species Awareness

Water hyacinth turns living wetlands into silent water deserts.

Fast-growing mats of floating plants choke lakes and rivers, cut off sunlight, drain oxygen, and push fish, birds, amphibians, and insects out of the habitats they depend on.

What changes first

The water surface becomes a barrier.

Water hyacinth spreads across the top of ponds, lakes, and slow rivers like a floating carpet. That blanket can trap heat, slow circulation, and make it harder for oxygen to enter the water.

Who feels it fastest

Fish and water birds lose room to feed.

Fish search for oxygen-rich pockets while birds struggle to reach open water. Frogs, turtles, insects, and the animals that prey on them are pulled into the same chain of disruption.

Why this matters

One invasive plant can destabilize an entire food web.
