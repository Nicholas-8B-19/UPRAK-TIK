Designed to turn awareness into action

This project presents the water hyacinth crisis as a visual story so viewers can quickly understand the danger invasive species pose to fish, birds, amphibians, and fragile wetland food webs.

Campaign vision

The goal of this website is simple: Make ecological damage feel visible, immediate, and human enough to care about. Instead of only listing facts, the site frames the issue as a habitat emergency affecting nearby animals right now.

Wildlife at risk

When water hyacinth spreads unchecked, nearby fish, birds, frogs, turtles, and aquatic insects all lose part of the habitat they need. The danger is not limited to one species because every layer of the wetland is connected.

Core message

Healthy ecosystems depend on balance. When one aggressive species dominates the surface, everything beneath it becomes harder to protect, from microscopic life to visible animals higher up the chain.

"Awareness is the first removal tool. Once people can see the damage clearly, action becomes easier to organize."
